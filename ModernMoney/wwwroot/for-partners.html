Discover the technology

Discover the technology

The Lykke marketplace uses the distributed ledger, which is blockchain technology pioneered by Bitcoin. A blockchain is a sequence of linked data records, or blocks. Each block refers to an earlier block in the chain, which strengthens the security of the entire sequence and makes it possible to store the data in a decentralized way, across multiple network-enabled devices in physical locations anywhere in the world. Using the blockchain as a ledger for financial transactions—as Bitcoin does, and as Lykke also does—all but eliminates the risk of fraud, because the ledger does not exist in any fixed place; rather, it exists in multiple places simultaneously, beyond the reach of any single authority.

One global market

Regulation and compliance are the biggest hurdles delaying widespread adoption of the crypto economy and digital markets. The regulatory landscape is quickly taking shape and Lykke is a part of this process. We work with regulators across the globe to introduce digital assets and operate trading infrastructure that will be fully compliant with securities market laws and regulatory standards. The Lykke Roadmap includes 14 regulated entities set to be operational in 2017/2018, serving the needs of investors on all continents.

Prospective regulated subsidiaries of Lykke:

  • Switzerland: FINMA-regulated organized trading facility (OTF),
  • United Kingdom: FCA-regulated multilateral trading facility (MTF),
  • Cyprus: CySEC-regulated broker/investment firm (CIF), CBC-regulated electronic money institution (EMI),
  • Singapore: MAS-regulated recognized market operator (RMO), MAS-regulated asset manager, MAS-regulated hedge fund, MAS-regulated stored value facility (SVF),
  • United States: SEC-regulated broker dealer (BD), alternative trading system (ATS) and registered investment advisor (RIA for wealth management); CFTC-regulated futures commission merchant (FCM) and designated contract market (DCM); Office Comptroller of Currency or State Regulator-regulated for banking license; jointly SEC- and CFTC-regulated hedge funds;
  • Vanuatu: VFSC-regulated dealer in securities

Integrated payment systems

Integrated payment systems

Lykke is building a payment infrastructure on blockchain that will facilitate faster growth and wider adoption of regulated cryptocurrencies around the world. Users that have access to the system will be able to pay with all available digital assets and the merchant will receive payment in the asset of their choice.

As we work on Lykke Pay, we are looking into the future when we believe micropayments on the blockchain will be available at every shop and restaurant, just as bank cards are used today.

A handheld POS terminal connected to Lykke Pay prints out a check with a QR code containing the recipient’s address and the amount to be paid in Bitcoins. As soon as Lykke Pay detects the required amount on the address provided, the terminal prints out a confirmation receipt.

While we are waiting for a payment from a customer we assume the market risk. Once the payment is received, merchants working with us can immediately convert the Bitcoins they receive to fiat currency using Lykke Exchange.
